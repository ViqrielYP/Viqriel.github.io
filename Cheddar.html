CHEDDAR

Cheddar cheese is a relatively hard cheese, pale yellow to ivory in color, and sometimes has a strong flavor. This cheese originates from the village of Cheddar in Somerset, England, and is the most popular cheese in the United Kingdom with a market share of around 51% of the UK's Â£1.9 billion annual cheese market. Cheddar cheese has been widely imitated, both in the UK, and in other countries such as the United States, Australia, New Zealand, South Africa, Canada and Iceland. In most countries in the world there are generally "Cheddar" cheeses available in various forms, ranging from low-fat Cheddar cheeses to aged, high-fat Cheddar cheeses. According to European trade standards, only cheese produced and originating from the four counties in South West England (Somerset, Devon, Dorset and Cornwall) can be labeled/labeled "West Country Farmhouse Cheddar" under the Protection of Indication of Origin (PDO). Cheddar cheese is also one of the most commonly available cheeses and is used in Indonesia as a cake and food ingredient.
