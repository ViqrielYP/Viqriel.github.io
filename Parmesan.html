PARMESAN

Parmesan cheese is an Italian cheese made from cow's milk. In terms of taste, parmesan cheese is a bit salty and has a fairly pungent aroma. According to the US Code of Federal Regulations, a cheese can be considered parmesan if it is made from cow's milk and has a gritty texture. In addition, the surface is hard and brittle and the age is about 10 months, as reported by All Recipes. See Photo Illustration of parmesan cheese. (PIXABAY/ ICB) Adding from the Healthline page, the name parmesan cheese is assigned directly by the European Union. Therefore, only cheeses made in certain locations can be called parmesan. These locations are the provinces of Parma, Reggio Emilia, Modena, Bologna and Mantua in Northern Italy.
