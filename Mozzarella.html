MOZZARELLA

Mozzarella is an Italian cheese made by turning and cutting, because in Italian, mozzare means cutting. This cheese is made from cow's milk or buffalo milk. Mozzarella di bufala is made from buffalo milk, while mozzarella fior di latte is made from pasteurized cow's milk. Fresh mozzarella is generally white in color, but can be bright yellow in color depending on the food the cow/buffalo feeds on. The texture of this cheese is soft and has a high water content. Generally, cheeses made on the same day are eaten on the same day. However, these cheeses can last up to a week when soaked in brine. Mozzarella with low moisture content can be stored in the refrigerator for up to a month, and can last longer. when sold in vacuum packaging. Mozzarella low water content that has been grated can be stored for up to 6 months. This cheese is used on pizza, lasagna, or eaten with sliced ​​tomatoes and basil in insalata caprese.
